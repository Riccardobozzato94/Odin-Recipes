Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish originating from Rome.
It features spaghetti or another type of pasta coated in a rich, creamy sauce made from eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta or guanciale (Italian cured meats), and black pepper.
The heat from the freshly cooked pasta helps to create a silky sauce without curdling the eggs. Carbonara is known for its simple yet indulgent flavor profile.

Ingredient

  • Pasta: Typically spaghetti, but fettuccine, rigatoni, or bucatini can also be used.
  • Pancetta or Guanciale: Italian cured meats; guanciale is traditional, but pancetta is commonly used.
  • Eggs: Usually just the yolks or a combination of whole eggs and yolks.
  • Pecorino Romano Cheese: Grated, though Parmesan can be used as a substitute or in combination.
  • Black Pepper: Freshly ground. Optional: Some recipes include a splash of heavy cream, though this is not traditional
